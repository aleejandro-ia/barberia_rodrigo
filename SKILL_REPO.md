## Categories

### Frontend / UI — Premium Landing Pages

**design-taste-frontend** (from Leonxlnx/taste-skill)
- What it does: Anti-slop frontend skill. Enforces premium design decisions, prevents AI tells (Inter font, 3-equal-card grids, AI-purple gradients, em-dashes). Has strict pre-flight checklist.
- When to use: Any landing page, portfolio, or marketing site that needs to feel agency-tier. Use when "premium" or "luxury" is in the brief.
- Source: `npx skills add Leonxlnx/taste-skill` → design-taste-frontend
- Quality notes: EXCELLENT. Very detailed, comprehensive rules. Prevents real LLM slop patterns. Use as BLOCKING skill for premium UI phases.

**high-end-visual-design** (from Leonxlnx/taste-skill)
- What it does: Agency-level visual design system. Enforces Double-Bezel components, custom cubic-bezier motion, floating glass nav, proper spatial rhythm.
- When to use: Pair with design-taste-frontend for maximum premium output on luxury brands.
- Source: `npx skills add Leonxlnx/taste-skill` → high-end-visual-design
- Quality notes: EXCELLENT. Use together with design-taste-frontend. Good motion choreography guidance.

**gpt-taste** (from Leonxlnx/taste-skill)
- What it does: AIDA structure enforcement, Python-driven layout randomization, GSAP scroll animations, gapless bento grids.
- When to use: Awwwards-tier experimental pages. Heavier GSAP usage than design-taste-frontend.
- Source: `npx skills add Leonxlnx/taste-skill` → gpt-taste
- Quality notes: GOOD for experimental agency work. More opinionated than design-taste-frontend. Use when brief explicitly wants GSAP scroll-hijack or bento grids.
